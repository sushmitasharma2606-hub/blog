I Don't Wanna Be One Thing

When I was young, I wrote a song. I didn't know what I was building — I just knew something felt too tight, like the future was a box someone else had measured for me.

I don't wanna be an artist, I don't wanna be a queen
I wanna be something in which I could be everything
Is it actual, or is it a dream?
I wanna be something in which I could be everything

Why can't I be multiple things at once? Why can't my identity be fluid?

Growing up, I watched everyone settle into an identity — one role, one path, one definition. But what happens to the rest of you? What are you when you step outside that role? Are those parts of you just background noise, or are they also you?

Society rewards clarity and labels. Ambiguity feels risky. So slowly, without realising it, I started choosing the safe version of myself — sticking to one lane, avoiding the unexplored sides because I was afraid that exploring them would confuse people. Or worse, confuse me.

But then a question lodged itself in my brain and refused to leave: Is it possible to build a version of myself that doesn't shrink me to one identity?

Here's what I've come to believe — your identity isn't a decision you make once. It's the sum of your small, daily actions. You don't wake up and choose who you are. You become who you are, one tiny choice at a time.

So here are the teeny tiny habits that are building me:

01
Document, don't just consume.

End every day with 3–4 lines written somewhere. I still have the scrappy, inconsistent list of poems I wrote as a teenager — and that messy collection is now one of my biggest proofs of creativity. Write it down, even badly.

02
Finish small things.

Not big projects — just closures. One post. One idea. One paragraph. One more hour. Small completions build the muscle of finishing, and finishing builds identity.

03
Have opinions, even imperfect ones.

Pick a side and say what you think — even when it's half-formed. Practising this tells your own mind: this is my building, and my voice matters here.

04
Only compare yourself to yourself.

Silent comparisons are poison. If something bothers you, become better than the version of you that it bothered. The metric by which you measure yourself is everything.

05
Create a daily task — and let its completion be enough.

That small win is your excellence for the day. Discomfort at the start of something new is normal. It means you're growing.

"I'm going to be an entrepreneur, a strong woman, a poet, a writer, a girl, and someone who never gives up — regardless of what my role description says."

I'm proud of who I'm becoming. And as a teaser — I've been quietly building something. A workspace brand rooted in Indian culture, started by two 19-year-olds with zero funds. The first collection is ready. The drop is coming.
